Food

Food

Provençal cuisine is a celebration of fresh, local ingredients and simple, bold flavors, deeply rooted in the region’s agricultural bounty and Mediterranean traditions. The region’s warm, sunny climate produces an abundance of sun-ripened vegetables like tomatoes, zucchini, eggplant, and peppers, along with fragrant herbs such as thyme, rosemary, basil, and lavender, known collectively as Herbes de Provence. Olives play a central role, whether enjoyed whole, pressed into silky olive oil, or blended into tapenade. Their farm-to-table philosophy ensures that every bite reflects the freshness and flavor of the season, making Provençal cuisine a true reflection of the land itself.

Markets, Boulangeries, Boucheries, and Charcuteries

In Provence, food is sourced largely from local markets and specialty shops, with residents preferring to buy fresh, high-quality products. Provençal people take pride in sourcing their ingredients from markets, which are the heart of every village and town. Here, you can find seasonal fruits, vegetables, herbs, and artisan products directly from local producers. In addition to markets, locals frequent boulangeries for freshly baked bread, boucheries for cuts of meat, and charcuteries for cured sausages and pâtés.

To truly immerse yourself in Provence's cuisine, shop locally! Food here is not just about sustenance, but about quality, tradition, and community.

Ratatouille

A rustic vegetable stew, ratatouille is a celebration of Provence’s sun-ripened produce. Made with a mix of tomatoes, zucchini, eggplant, peppers, and herbs, the vegetables are slowly cooked to meld the flavors into a rich, comforting dish. Often served as a side or main, it’s a perfect reflection of Provençal simplicity and seasonal freshness.

Bouillabaisse

A traditional Provençal fish stew from Marseille, bouillabaisse is a hearty dish made with a variety of fresh local fish, shellfish, and aromatic herbs, all simmered together in a rich, saffron-infused broth. It’s typically served with rouille, a garlicky mayonnaise, and crusty bread, making it a flavorful and unforgettable meal that captures the essence of the Mediterranean.

Socca

A beloved street food from Nice, socca is a thin, crispy chickpea flour pancake, traditionally cooked in a wood-fired oven. It has a slightly nutty flavor and is best enjoyed hot, sprinkled with a dash of black pepper. Simple yet satisfying, socca makes for a perfect snack or light meal, offering a taste of the Côte d'Azur’s vibrant food culture.
