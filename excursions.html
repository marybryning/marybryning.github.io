Excursions

Provence offers a wealth of incredible excursions, from swimming in the crystal-clear waters of the Calanques, to hiking through the dramatic landscapes of the Gorges du Verdon, and wandering through the fragrant, purple-hued lavender fields that stretch as far as the eye can see. There really is something for everyone! To make the most of what this majestic region has to offer, it is recommended to hire a car as some areas can be difficult to access by public transport.

Nice and the Côte d'Azur

One of the most well connected areas in Provence, in Nice and the Côte d'Azur adventure awaits around every corner. Start your exploration with a dip in the sea at sunrise, or on the stunning Nice to Villefranche coastal walk, where you’ll enjoy breathtaking views of the Mediterranean coastline and charming seaside villages. Venture to Menton in late February to discover the vibrant hues of its citrus festival, and don’t miss the glamour of Monaco, where you can marvel at the opulent casino and stroll the luxurious harbor.

The best way to explore these charming seaside towns is with Nice's stunning seaside railway network, with train tickets costing only 2-7€ (depending on destination).

Marseille and the Calanques

The oldest city in France, Marseille offers an abundance of activities for history and culture lovers alike! Explore the iconic Notre-Dame de la Garde for panoramic city views, immerse yourself in history at Mucem and Fort Saint-Jean, wander through the bustling Vieux Port and admire the boats, or lose yourself in the charm of the Old Town with its vibrant markets and quaint streets.

Along the coast from Marseille, the Calanques offer breathtaking natural beauty. Start your adventure in Cassis and hike to the Calanque de Port-Miou to admire the picturesque boats nestled in the bay. Continue your trek to the stunning Calanque de Port Pin and the dramatic Calanque d’En Vau, with the journey ranging from 30 minutes to 1.5 hours depending on your destination. Be sure to pack plenty of water and wear footwear with good grip, as the rocky paths can be slippery. The rugged coastline and crystal-clear waters make this hike a memorable experience.

Valensole

Visit Valensole in late June to early July to experience the breathtaking beauty of its lavender fields in full bloom. This picturesque village in Provence transforms into a sea of vibrant purple stretching as far as the eye can see. Don't forget to visit the charming lavender gift shop to treat yourself or your loved ones to something special!

Beyond the lavender, Valensole offers charming streets, local markets, and a peaceful, rustic atmosphere that captures the essence of Provençal life. It's the perfect time to immerse yourself in the region's natural beauty and tranquil ambiance.

Les Gorges du Verdon

For an unforgettable adventure in the Gorges du Verdon, start your journey at Quinson and make your way up the gorge to Esparron. This stunning route showcases the dramatic cliffs and crystal-clear waters of one of France’s most beautiful canyons. At Location Nautic, you can rent pedalos, kayaks, or electric boats from 20-60€ to explore the gorge at your own pace, making it an ideal activity for families and friends. Along the way, take advantage of the turquoise waters to stop for a refreshing swim and soak in the natural beauty of this spectacular landscape.
